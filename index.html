HOW THIS WORKS

Some screens stay dark until you wake them. Look for the glowing emblems and activate the right one:

ORANGE / YELLOW flame GREEN shoot

Solve all six stations. Four of them give you a ★ code number. Those four unlock the FINAL CODE at the bottom. Type your answers right here as you go — your final code fills in automatically. Good luck, explorer.

STATION 1The Glass Dome
orangegreen
A glass dome holds a falling rain,
where flight and forest meet again.
Touch the twin marks of gold and green —
only then is the secret seen.
First, find the building this dome belongs to. Then activate both emblems to reveal the hidden words and fill in the blank:
"Singapore's Changi Airport is one of the busiest in the world, with a plane landing or taking off every ______ seconds."
★This is CODE NUMBER 1 — it flows down to the final lock automatically.
STATION 2The Oath
phone needed
Two men, hands raised, a solemn vow;
a crowd looks on — a nation, now.
No caption here to give the date —
your phone may help you investigate.
This relief shows the swearing-in of Singapore's first Prime Minister and his cabinet — the nation's first step toward self-government. Use your phone: in what year did this oath-taking ceremony take place?
★This is CODE NUMBER 2.
STATION 3From the Ashes
orangegreenphone needed
Two screens hold one story. Wake the orange flame,
then the green shoot, and watch a city fall — and rise again.
PART A — THE FIRE (orange emblem)
"Japanese attacks from the air, sea and land culminated in the fall of Singapore in the year ______."
★This is CODE NUMBER 3.
PART B — THE GREEN SHOOT (green emblem)
Read the screen and fill in the blank:
"The people of Singapore rallied to build a nation, labouring in the urgent construction of homes, ____________, and infrastructure."
PART C — A MESSAGE OF PEACE
From 1983 to 2022, Daisaku Ikeda, President of the Soka Gakkai International, wrote a peace proposal every year — forty in all. He taught that lasting peace is built not through treaties alone but through a transformation in the human heart: choosing dialogue, respect for the dignity of every life, and the courage of ordinary people — especially youth — to act.
Search ikeda.org to complete his words:
"Nothing is more ____________ than war. Nothing is more cruel."
And on the weapons that threaten all humanity, he taught that the real enemy is not the weapons themselves, but the readiness to ____________ others who are seen as a threat — which is why he devoted himself to the ____________ of nuclear weapons.
Parts B & C are words to think about and discuss — no code numbers here.
STATION 4The Living River
orange
Find the river where bumboats crowd,
their tyres like necklaces, hulls low with cargo.
Behind the stone wall stand the tall shophouses, three storeys high,
where bullock carts and rickshaws pass.

Wake the orange flame, and the walls turn to glass —
a doll's-house view of life within.
Climb with your eyes to the highest floor on the left.
The day's hard labour is done; bodies lie still, eyes closed.
What are the people on the third floor (left) doing?
★CODE NUMBER 4 = the position of your letter (A=1, B=2, C=3, D=4).
STATION 5Pose Like a Crab!
photo time
You've reached the journey's end, where a giant of blue and orange fills the wall —
claws raised, eyes on stalks, ruler of the mangrove glow.

No words to find here, no number to crack.
Just face the screen, raise your arms like mighty pincers, and freeze.
Strike your best crab pose with the creature behind you — and capture the moment. Snap! 📸
No code number — pure glory.
STATION 6The Courage Within
phone needed
Wait for the crab to fade, and watch the night sky
stitch its stars into a mane and a roar.

The lion is no ordinary beast — it is the king, and it fears nothing.
The Soka Gakkai teaches that its courage is not far away in the stars,
but already inside you.
In Buddhism, the lion is a symbol of courage. Search sokaglobal.org to complete the words of Nichiren Daishonin:
"Each of you should summon up the courage of a ______ ______ and never succumb to threats from anyone."
A reflection to carry with you — no code number.
